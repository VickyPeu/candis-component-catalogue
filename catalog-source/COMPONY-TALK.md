# Compony — AI Adoption Biweekly (10 min talk)

**Goal:** show what Compony is, the problem it solves, and the lesson learned — to a mixed,
mostly non-marketing/design audience. Keep it concrete, low jargon. **Target 10 min** (hard cap 15).

Flow: Problem → our process → why Contentful gets complex → quick pillars → live demo → the honest
lesson. One beat = roughly one slide. Talking points are prompts, not a script to read.

---

### 1 · Title (~15s)
**Compony — so finden wir die richtigen Webseiten-Bausteine (statt zu raten).**
_The Candis website component catalogue + an AI recommender._
- "I'm from Design/UX. I built a small AI tool that helps us build website pages faster. Here's the story in 10 minutes."

### 2 · The problem (~1.5 min)
**Building a Candis web page = picking from 30+ Contentful building blocks — and knowing which one fits.**
- A page (e.g. a product or industry page) is assembled from ready-made blocks: heroes, feature grids, testimonial sliders, CTA sections…
- If you weren't there when those blocks were built, you don't know: which ones exist, which fits your goal, how much text it allows, what images it needs.
- Result: PMM + copywriting lose time, guess, or pick the wrong block — and find out late.
- _Say it plainly:_ "The building blocks exist — the hard part is *finding the right one and knowing its rules*."

### 3 · Our process, very roughly (~1 min)
**Define the page → pick the blocks in Contentful → fill in copy → drop in design assets.**
- Marketing/PMM decide what a page should say; copy writes it; design delivers the visuals; it's assembled in Contentful (our CMS).
- The recurring bottleneck is one step: **"which block do I use, and what are its limits?"** — that's the bit that needs tribal knowledge.

### 4 · Why Contentful gets complex (~2 min)
**Many blocks, but the CMS limits how many entry types we can have — so we use _variants_.**
- One "component" often has many modes packed into it. Example: a single **Grid Section** has **16 variants** (different bento sizes, logo sliders, YouTube grids, a CTA layout…).
- That's efficient for the system, but **opaque for people**: same name, very different behaviour, with hidden rules (char limits, required images, aspect ratios).
- _Punchline:_ "It's powerful if you built it — and a maze if you didn't."

### 5 · The pillars, in 30 seconds (~1 min)
**Everything we say rests on 5 messaging pillars** — so the right block also depends on the *message*.
- Trust Anchor · Translation of Complexity · Standard Setter · Thought Leader · Authority.
- Each block tends to serve certain pillars (e.g. a testimonial slider = Trust Anchor; a video-tab demo = Trust + Translation of Complexity).
- Why it matters for the tool: Compony matches **your goal + pillar** to the blocks that fit.

### 6 · Compony — live demo (~3 min) ⭐ the centerpiece
**Type what you want to say → get the right blocks, why, the rules, and a wireframe.**
- Pick ONE concrete example, e.g. _"show our partner logos with a short explanation of each partnership."_
- Walk through what comes back:
  - the matching component(s) + a tailored "why",
  - the **copy limits** and **required assets** (no guessing),
  - a **wireframe** (click to enlarge — show the lightbox), and a **link straight into Contentful**.
- Maybe one more quick brief to show it reasons, not keyword-matches (e.g. a German brief).
- _Keep moving — the demo should feel fast and a little fun (Compony the unicorn loads with playful lines)._

### 7 · The honest lesson — scope (~1.5 min)
**I'd tried this before with big ideas. It never shipped. What worked: cutting scope hard.**
- Earlier attempts wanted to do everything → too complex → never finished.
- The unlock was one question: **"What do we specifically need, right now, today?"**
- Answer: just "help pick the right block + show its rules." Once scoped that tight, **the tool stood within a day** (with AI doing the heavy lifting).
- _Takeaway for the room:_ start narrow, ship something real, expand later.

### 8 · Close (~30s)
**Start small, ship, grow.** Compony already covers 31 components and is live for the team to try.
- "Happy to give anyone a walkthrough or adapt the idea for your team's tools."
